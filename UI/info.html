Who we are

We are a team of five undergraduate students, who are majoring in Computer Science at the University of Massachusetts in Boston. We are hard-working students that chose a very challenging but exciting project to work on. This project brought us together to collaborate and work as team. Our purpose is to make a successful scanning tool that will meet the needs and expectations of our client. We design this tool to help with the penotyping of the calcific atherosclerosis in the carotid artery. However, this tool can also be used for other medical purposes.

Our tool

A browser-based semi-automatic tool that identifies morphologic features of carotid atherosclerotic calcifications. The current goal of this project and tool is based around allowing users to identify areas of calcific plaque build-up and calculating morphologic features of the build up based on the user annotation. The user will be able to use the brush tool to paint on the medical images and get those annotations by a single click on the display button. The user can use the plus and minus button to change the size of the brush. They also have the opportunity to pick between 5 colors (red, green, blue, purple, and pink). There is also a button that clears all the brush annotations on the DICOM image. In case the user does not want to clear all the brush annotations, they have the choice to undo single brush strokes. Moreover, if a brush stroke has been deleted by mistake there is a redo button that will bring back that brush stroke. Other important tools are the zoom button, which allows for expansion or diminution, the pan button, which allows to move the image around. Last but not least, there is the length button. With the length button, the user can take the length between two points on the medical image. The purpose for this button is to find the distance of the calcific plaque from the lumen.

How to use the scanner

Buttons Descriptions
Brush Tool: Press on this button to paint on the DICOM images. When pressing on this button the increase and decrease of the brush size, as well as, the brush color buttons and choices are enabled.
Brush size: Press on this button to increase the size of your brush.
Brush size: Press on this button to decrease the size of your brush.
Brush-Color Press on this button to change the color of your brush. The color choices are red, green, purple, blue and pink. The default color for the brush is red.
Clear Brush Button: Press on this button to clear any brush annotation on the DICOM image.
Undo Brush Button: Press on this button to undo your brush strokes one by one.
Redo Brush Button: Press on this button to redo a brush stroke that you may have deleted by mistake.
Zoom Button: Press on this button to enable the zoom button. When pressing on the zoom button the brush size, and the brush color buttons are disabled. To enable them again press on the brush button.
Pan Button: Press on this button to allow movement of your image when there is zoom in applied.
Length Button: Press on this button to take the length between two points. This tool is designed to take the distance of a calcific plaque from the lumen. However, if this tool is being used for other medical occasions, the user can still take the length between two points.
Display Statistics Button: Press on this button to display on the right side of the image viewer the statistics that have been calculated by painting on the DICOM image. Those statistics include count of brush strokes (in this occasion for calcific plaques), the area the brush stroke is covering, the pixel density of that area, and the volume. These statistics are being printed according to the chosen color.

Clinic Relevance

Carotid artherosclerotic plaque in the neck is a common cause of ischemic stroke in the brain. Atherosclerosis is the deposition of inflamed fat in the vessel walls that lead to hardening of the arteries. Hardened arteries with inflammation can lead to unstable plaque, which can break into pieces and travel and obstruct blood vessels in the brain. Lack ofoxygenated blood flow causes strokes. Understanding plaque features that lead to instability are, therefore, absolutely critical for doctors to identify patients at risk for stroke. Calcific plaque is fascinating as it may have dual roles in both plaque stability and instability. This project involves characterizing qualitative and quantitative morphologic features of calcific plaque. Using noninvasive diagnostic neuroimaging exams such as computed tomographic angiography (CTA) to image the blood vessels, we identify patients with diseased vessels with atherosclerotic plaque. Our client team includes neuroradiologists, vascular neurologists, and vascular surgeons at the University of Pennsylvania.
Scientific Definitions
Terms Definitions
CCS Carotid Calcium Scanner
DICOM Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine. Type of input file used by CCS.
CT A Computed Tomography scan combines a series of X-ray images taken from different angles around your body and uses computer processing to create cross-sectional images (slices) of the bones, blood vessels and soft tissues inside your body
CTA Computed Tomography Angiography
PET Positron Emission Tomography scan
Plaque The buildup of fats, cholesterol and other potentially harmful substances in and/or on the artery walls
Atherosclerosis Thickening or hardening of the artery caused by a build up of plaque on the inner artery lining
Calcifications Calcium that has built up in a body tissue, which causes it to harden. Calcification of the arteries causes the arterial wall to stiffen and become inflamed, which can result in chunks of calcified plaque to break off and obstruct blood flow to the brain
Cerebrovascular Ischemic Event A term for when the flow of blood to the brain is reduced to a point where it cannot meet its metabolic demand. This leads to a reduction in oxygen flow to the brain, death of brain tissue, stroke, or death
Stroke the sudden death of brain cells due to a lack of oxygen. The lack of oxygen is generally due to a blockage of blood flow or rupture of an artery connected to the brain
Lumen The cavity or channel within a tube or tubular organ such as a blood vessel or the intestine
Stenosis An abnormal narrowing of a channel in the body; in this case, referring to narrowing of the artery due to atherosclerosis
